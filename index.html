    • Semester One: Earth Science Study Guide Answers (Astronomy)

      • 1. What are meters, liters, and grams each a measurement of?
          The Metric System
      • 2. 1000m = ____mm
          1,000,000mm
      • 3. 1000m - ____km
          7km
      • 4. 4.6cL = ____L
          .046L
      • 5. .0078kL = ____mL
          7800mL
      • 6. 34cg = ____mg
          340mg
      • 7. .55mg = ____g
          .055g


      • 8. What is density?
          The degree of compactness of a substance
      • 9. What is the calculation of density?
          Mass x Volume
      • 10. If a rock has a mass of 45 g and a volume of 15cm3, what is the rocks density?
          3g/cm3
      • 11. If a hot dog has a density of 10g/cm3 and a mass of 20g, what is the hot dogs volume?
          200cm3


        • 12. What is a star?
            A fixed luminous point in the night sky that is a large, remote incandescent body like the sun.
        • 13. What causes the sun to shine?
            It shines from Nuclear Fusion
        • 14. Describe the sizes of stars.
            Many sizes of stars differ from the type of stars they are. Neutron stars, black dwarfs, white dwarfs, brown dwarfs, red giants,and red supergiants.
        • 15. What is the sun?
            The Sun is a Star
        • 16. How much of the sun is hydrogen and how much of it is helium?
            Around 75% Hydrogen and 25% Helium
        • 17. List the Five main colors of the stars
            Red, Orange, Yellow, Blue, White
        • 18. What colored stars are the hottest? What are the coolest?
            Blue stars are the hottest. Red stars are the coolest.
        • 19. What color is the sun? What colors can Main Sequence stars be?
            Our Sun is Yellow. They can be Red, Orange, Yellow, White, Blue
        • 20. Where would you find the Sun in the H-R Diagram?
            You would find the Sun around the middle.
        • 21. Where would you find red giants/super-giants in the H-R Diagram?
            You can find them in the Upper Right.
        • 22. What 3 main things determine a star's brightness?
            Size, color, and distance
        • 23. What is the scientific name for brightness? Do brighter stars have a higher or lower number on the brightness scale?
            Magnitude. Brighter stars have a lower number.
        • 24. What is the difference between absolute and apparent magnitude?
            Absolute Magnitude is the actual brightness measured at 10 parsecs away. Apparent magnitude is the brightness from Earth.
        • 25. How is it possible for a star to have temperature of 3,000K and have the same absolute magnitude as a star with a temperature of 30,000K?
            The mass and the actual distance.
        • 26. Most of the stars in the sky are in what stage of their life cycle??
            They are in their Main Sequence phase.
        • 27. What are nebulas?
            A cloud of gas and dust in outer space, visible in the night sky either as an indistinct bright patch or as a dark silhouette against other luminous matter.
        • 28. What is the first stage of a star's life cycle called?
            Nebulae
        • 29. Do stars change over time??
            They change by them running out of fuel and expanding.
        • 30. What single property will determines the way a star will change over time?
            It's mass determine's the cycle.
        • 31. List the life stages of a low/medium mass star.
            Protostar, Main Sequence, Red Giant, White Dwarf
        • 32. List the life stages of a high mass star.
            Protostar, Main Sequence, Red Super Giant, Super Nova, Neutron Star/Black Hole.
        • 33. About how old is the sun?
            The Sun is 4.5 Billion Years Old

          almost as old as your mom

        • 34. About how much longer will the Sun remain the same size and color?
            Around 4.5 Billion Years more.
        • 35. What is the next stage in the Sun's cycle? When will the Sun move out of it's current stage and what will happen to it during it's next stage?
            Red Giant. It will turn into one in 4.5 billion years because it will be running out of fuel and will start to expand
        • 36. What is a galaxy?
            A system of millions or billions of stars, together with gas and dust, held together by gravitational attraction.
        • 37. How many stars does a galaxy typically contain?
            Approximately 100 billion stars
        • 38. What are the three type of galaxies?
            Irregular, Spiral, Elliptical
        • 39. Where is our Solar System located?
            Our Solar System is located in an arm halfway out of the Milky Way Galaxy
        • 40. What is the approximate diameter of the Milky Way?
            100,000 light years
        • 41. Describe the Milky Way's appearance from Earth.
            White cloud of stars in a line
        • 42. Earth and Moon rocks indicate that our Solar System formed how many years ago?
            Approximately 4.6 billion years ago
        • 43. Name the Eight planets in order.
             Mercury, Venus, Earth, Mars, Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus, Neptune
        • 44. Name the 4 outer planets. What are their characteristics?
            Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus, Neptune. They are Gas Planets.
        • 45. Name the 4 inner planets. What are their characteristics?
             Mercury, Venus, Earth, Mars. They are terrestial planets.
        • 46. Explain how scientists believe the moon was created.
            Debris from a collision
        • 47. Is the universe still expanding?
            Yes
        • 48. Describe the motion of all distant galaxies.
            Spiralling around in circles
        • 49. How do we know the direction in which Galaxies are moving?
            Radial Motion and Proper Motion
        • 50. Why do distance galaxies have red appearance?
            They are moving away
        • 51. If we could "rewind" the universe, describe the motion of the galaxy?
            They would be closer together.
        • 52. How long ago were all the galaxies, and the entire universe, contained in a single point?
            Around 13.7 billion years ago.
